🍄 Lion's Mane Mushroom — Hericium erinaceus

In: Mushroom Extract Complex →

Lion's Mane contains hericenones and erinacines — compounds that stimulate Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) synthesis in the brain. Multiple human clinical trials show improvements in mild cognitive impairment, focus, and memory.

Phytotherapy Research · 2009 · Mori K, et al.

Improving effects of the mushroom Yamabushitake (Hericium erinaceus) on mild cognitive impairment

Subjects taking Lion's Mane for 16 weeks showed significantly higher cognitive function scores vs placebo. Scores declined after discontinuation.

International Journal of Medicinal Mushrooms · 2013 · Lai PL, et al.

Neurotrophic properties of the Lion's mane medicinal mushroom, Hericium erinaceus, from Malaysia

Lion's Mane extracts demonstrated neurotrophic activity, stimulating neurite outgrowth — the mechanism behind its nerve-growth-factor reputation.

Read on PubMed →
Biomedical Research · 2010 · Nagano M, et al.

Reduction of depression and anxiety by 4 weeks Hericium erinaceus intake

Participants consuming Lion's Mane for 4 weeks reported significantly reduced depression and anxiety scores compared with the placebo group.

A review of Shilajit's constituents and traditional use at altitude, covering fatigue, hypoxia tolerance and energy metabolism.

💧 Methylene Blue — Methylthioninium chloride

In: Methylene Blue Drops →

Methylene Blue is a mitochondrial electron carrier with over 150 years of pharmaceutical use. Modern research shows it acts as an alternative electron acceptor in the mitochondrial electron transport chain, enhancing ATP production and cognitive performance.

Pharmacology Biochemistry and Behavior · 2004 · Callaway NL, et al.

Methylene blue improves brain oxidative metabolism and memory retention in rats

A single low dose of methylene blue raised brain cytochrome oxidase activity and improved memory retention in rats — the foundational animal work behind its metabolic-enhancer reputation.

Read on PubMed →
Progress in Neurobiology · 2012 · Rojas JC, et al.

Neurometabolic mechanisms for memory enhancement and neuroprotection of methylene blue

A review of how low-dose methylene blue enhances mitochondrial respiration, and the neurometabolic basis for its memory and neuroprotective effects.

Read on PubMed →
Biochemical Pharmacology · 2014 · Gonzalez-Lima F, et al.

Mitochondrial respiration as a target for neuroprotection and cognitive enhancement

Reviews mitochondrial respiration as a therapeutic target, with low-dose methylene blue as the lead metabolic enhancer for cognition and neuroprotection.
